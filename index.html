Lisa Chen

Hello, I'm Lisa Chen.

I am going into my fourth year at the University of Toronto, where I major in the History of Science and Technology and double minor in Computer Science and Communications. My background in the humanities and the technical knowledge I have gained from my CS courses uniquely places me at the intersection of science and the arts, which enables me to think creatively and logically. I am interested in UI, web development and digital strategy.

Check out my work


This is a mobile app I prototyped for IBM'S Intern Challenge. SGI Drive Safe is for Saskatchewan Government Insurance's Auto Fund. There is a version for each of the 3 stakeholders:

Students: uses telematics and analytics to track driving behavior and returns insights on how students can improve their driving

Instructors: access analytics of individual students to gain insight on their strengths and weaknesses. This will help instructors create personalized lesson plans

Examiners: digitizes the pen and paper exam process and allows examiners to drop pins on Google map API in real time when infractions occur. Data is sent to SGI for analysis instead of filed away in a cabinet

Technology used: Adobe Photoshop, InVision

The Chubby Chaser
The Chubby Chaser is a game I made for a school project where our hero, Gingerfreddy, goes through various rooms in a house to look for his next meal. There are three levels: breakfast, lunch and dinner. All the ingredients Gingerfreddy must find are greyed out at the bottom, and once he retrieves the item it will appear in full colour.

Technology used: Java
